[COURSE MATERIAL: HISTORY 101 - UNIT 5]
TOPIC: THE CAUSES OF THE GREAT DEPRESSION

1. THE STOCK MARKET CRASH (1929)
- "Buying on Margin": Investors could buy stock with 10% down. This created an asset bubble.
- Black Tuesday (Oct 29, 1929): The crash itself destroyed $30 billion in wealth, but was just the trigger, not the sole cause.

2. STRUCTURAL WEAKNESSES
- Industry: Key industries like railroads and steel were already struggling.
- Agriculture: Farmers were suffering from overproduction and low prices throughout the 1920s (Dust Bowl conditions worsened this later).
- Wealth Inequality: The top 1% possessed 40% of the wealth, limiting aggregate demand.

3. POLICY FAILURES
- Hawley-Smoot Tariff (1930): Raised tariffs to record levels, causing a trade war that reduced global trade by 66%.
- Federal Reserve Policy: The Fed RAISED interest rates in 1931 to preserve the gold standard, instead of lowering them to support banks. This contraction of the money supply is widely regarded by economists (like Milton Friedman) as the primary cause of the depth of the depression.

4. KEY DISCREPANCIES TO WATCH FOR
- Students often confuse the 1929 crash with the entire depression.
- Students must mention the banking panics.
