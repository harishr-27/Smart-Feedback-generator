The Great Depression started in 1929 when the stock market crashed. This event is called Black Tuesday. Many people had invested their money in stocks and lost everything overnight.

Another reason was that farmers were having trouble. They grew too much food and couldn't sell it for a good price. This hurt the economy because farmers make up a big part of the country.

The government tried to help but made some mistakes. They raised taxes on imported goods which made things worse. Eventually, World War 2 helped end the depression because everyone got jobs making supplies for the war.

In conclusion, the depression had many causes like the stock market and farming issues.
